Why More Kelowna Seniors Are Choosing Mobile Foot Care

By Lisa Tattersall, LPN, FCN · 6 min read

For years, foot care meant finding a salon, arranging a ride, waiting in a busy waiting room, and hoping the technician was gentle. For many Kelowna seniors, that chain of events is simply no longer realistic. Mobile foot care changes everything.

The problem with "just going to a salon"

Let's be honest about what a salon pedicure asks of a senior:

  • Getting into and out of a car — often difficult with arthritis, balance issues, or post-surgery recovery
  • Waiting in a chair that may not be supportive or comfortable
  • Climbing into a pedicure chair that's elevated off the ground
  • Dealing with strong chemical smells, loud dryers, and bright lights
  • Navigating technicians who aren't trained in safe care for thinning skin, blood thinners, or diabetic feet

For someone at 80, that's not relaxing. It's exhausting, and sometimes genuinely unsafe.

What mobile foot care actually looks like

When I visit a senior client at home, here's what happens:

  • I arrive at the agreed time with everything needed — basin, towels, sterile tools, and products
  • You stay in your favourite chair. No standing, no transfers, no awkward positions.
  • I set up on the floor in front of you, protecting your carpet or flooring
  • Your feet soak in warm water while we chat
  • I trim, file, moisturise, and polish if you'd like — with full clinical attention
  • When we're done, I clean everything up and leave no trace

Most visits take 45 to 60 minutes. You never leave your home.

The clinical benefit most people don't realise

Here's what I think is most important about mobile foot care for seniors: it's not just a pedicure. It's a regular health check.

Every visit, I look at skin integrity, circulation, nail health, callus formation, and signs of underlying issues like fungal infections or peripheral neuropathy. Many of my senior clients are on blood thinners, have diabetes, or have conditions that affect their feet. When something changes, I notice — often before anyone else does.

More than once, I've been the one who spotted something serious and referred a client to their doctor in time. That's not something a typical salon pedicurist is trained to do.

What about assisted living and care facilities?

I regularly visit assisted living homes and long-term care facilities across the Kelowna area. If you're a family member of a resident, or you manage a facility, I can:

  • Visit multiple residents in a single trip (often with a small group discount)
  • Coordinate with staff around medication schedules and other appointments
  • Keep clinical notes that can be shared with care teams
  • Set up a recurring monthly or bi-monthly schedule

Comfort matters as much as care

I'll say something obvious but worth saying: many of my senior clients haven't had their feet properly looked after in years. Some are embarrassed. Some have been brushed off by pedicurists who don't know what to do with thickened nails or damaged skin.

I'm never judgemental. Your feet might be the result of decades of work, walking, and living — they deserve respect, not a lecture.
